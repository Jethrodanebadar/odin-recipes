Pork Adobo

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Process

  1. In a large bowl, combine the pork cubes, sliced onions, minced garlic, soy sauce, and peppercorns.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the pork cubes, sliced onions, minced garlic, soy sauce, and peppercorns.
  3. Mix well, ensuring that the pork is evenly coated with the marinade.
  4. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.
  5. In a wide, heavy-bottomed pan or pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  6. Add the marinated pork mixture to the pan.
  7. Sear the pork pieces until they are brown on all sides. This helps enhance the flavor of the dish.
  8. Pour in the vinegar and allow it to boil for a few minutes to get rid of its strong smell.
  9. Add water, bay leaves, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  10. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 40-50 minutes or until the pork becomes tender. Stir occasionally.
  11. Taste the adobo and adjust the seasoning by adding more soy sauce, vinegar, or pepper according to your preference.
  12. Continue to simmer until the sauce thickens to your liking.
  13. Once the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened, remove the pot from heat.
  14. Serve the Pork Adobo hot over steamed rice.
