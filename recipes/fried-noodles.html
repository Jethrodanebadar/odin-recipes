Fried noodles

Ingredients

  • 250g Hong Kong-style egg noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup sliced onions
  • 1/2 cup julienned carrots
  • 1/2 cup sliced bell peppers (a mix of colors)
  • 1/2 cup sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Process

  1. Cook the Hong Kong-style egg noodles according to the package instructions.
  2. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to stop the cooking process.
  3. Toss the noodles with a bit of oil to prevent sticking and set aside.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the light soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and sesame oil. Set aside.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add minced garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant but not browned.
  7. Add sliced onions, julienned carrots, sliced bell peppers, and shiitake mushrooms.
  8. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  9. Add the cooked and drained noodles to the wok.
  10. Toss the noodles with the vegetables to combine evenly.
  11. Pour the prepared sauce over the noodles and vegetables.
  12. Use chopsticks or tongs to gently toss everything together until well coated.
  13. Add bean sprouts and continue tossing for an additional 1-2 minutes until the bean sprouts are slightly wilted.
  14. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  15. Transfer the Hong Kong-style fried noodles to a serving dish.
  16. Garnish with chopped green onions.
