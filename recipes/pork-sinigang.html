Pork Adobo

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 radish, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bunch string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2 pieces green chili peppers (siling haba)
  • 1 medium-sized taro (gabi), peeled and sliced
  • 1 packet (about 40g) sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 2 liters water
  • Fish sauce or salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste

Process

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
  2. Add the pork belly and onions. Simmer until the pork is tender (approximately 30-45 minutes), skimming off any scum that rises to the surface.
  3. Dissolve the sinigang mix in a cup of water. Strain the tamarind juice into the pot with the pork.
  4. Add the tomatoes, eggplant, radish, string beans, green chili peppers, and taro to the pot. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season the sinigang with fish sauce or salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.
  6. Once the pork and vegetables are tender, and the soup is well-seasoned, it's ready to serve.
